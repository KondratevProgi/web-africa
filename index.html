The Swahili word for “zebra” is “pundamilia,” which translates literally to “striped donkey.” The function of a zebra’s stripes is still up for debate, though it is widely thought that the stripes act as a way of confusing predators, either by camouflaging or making the zebra’s form ambiguous from a distance.
